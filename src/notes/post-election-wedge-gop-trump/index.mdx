Seems to me there are some misaligned incentives for senior Republicans on the matter of handling Trump from here on out, with all the flailing we should expect in the face of defeat.

Some no doubt see it in their interest to prop him up until the end. Ted Cruz and Lindsay Graham seem to have made this calculation. I figure they see the Trump base as their most motivated supporters likely to keep them relevant in the days to come.

Cruz likely runs again in 2024 and steers predictably to the hard right. Graham’s angle, I think, may be more about what’s in the best interest of the party. The last thing they need is an erratic Trump seeing the GOP as the enemy in his waning hours and sabotaging them on the way out the door. That’s the only reason I can figure he would light a million on fire for the Trump legal charades. Neither Graham nor Cruz actually like the man very much.

I think McConnell has made a different calculus. If I were in his brain, I’d probably think that I got what I needed out of Trump, and from here out it’s diminishing returns. Best to wash my hands of it and focus on the task ahead. 2022 will present many pickup opportunities in the senate and governor races, and you’ll be amazed by how fast the tide turns on Trump even within the GOP after they’ve decided he’s a loser they can’t afford to gamble with.

McConnell is a purely political creature through and through, and he’s offered no words of encouragement for team Trump so far. I think Liz Cheney is making similar calculations for the House.

Ron Johnson and Pat Toomey are up in 2022 in Wisconsin and Pennsylvania respectively. They can’t win on Trump voters alone, but they lose if the base stays home. Expect plenty of hedging and the occasional “strong” statement against Trump’s more extreme actions. They’re probably better off without him politically as 2022 will probably be more favorable to the GOP overall, if history is the guide.

It’s easy to look at the last 4 years and think of these guys as spineless go-along Trump enablers, and yeah…I get it. But they’re about self-preservation more than anything else, and the second that Trump no longer benefits them they are out. Seems like it’s just a matter of when and how loud they can afford to be.
